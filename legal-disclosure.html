Client Relationship Disclosures

Important information about our client relationships, agency duties, and service limitations.

Agency Relationship

Fiduciary Duty: We represent our clients' best interests and maintain confidentiality throughout the transaction process. Our primary duty is to our clients' success and satisfaction.

Confidentiality: All client information is kept strictly confidential and is not disclosed to third parties without explicit consent, except as required by law.

Dual Agency: In cases where we represent both buyer and seller, this relationship will be disclosed in writing, and both parties must consent to the arrangement.

Commission Structure

Commission Rates: Our commission rates are competitive and disclosed upfront. No hidden fees or surprise charges will be imposed.

Payment Terms: Commission is typically paid at closing and is based on the final sale price or rental amount as agreed in the listing agreement.

Additional Costs: Any additional costs for marketing, photography, or other services will be discussed and approved before implementation.

Conflict of Interest

Disclosure Policy: We disclose any potential conflicts of interest and maintain transparency in all dealings. This includes personal interests in properties or relationships with other parties.

Professional Independence: Our recommendations are based on professional judgment and market analysis, not personal financial interests.

Third-Party Relationships: Any relationships with lenders, inspectors, or other service providers are disclosed to clients.

Service Limitations

Scope of Services: Our services are provided to the best of our professional ability within the scope of real estate brokerage and property management.

Third-Party Services: We may recommend third-party services (legal, inspection, financing) but are not responsible for their performance or outcomes.

Market Conditions: We cannot guarantee market conditions, property values, or transaction outcomes, as these are subject to market forces beyond our control.

Property Disclosures

Property Condition

Information Accuracy: We provide accurate property information to the best of our knowledge. However, buyers are encouraged to conduct independent inspections and due diligence.

Property Inspections: We recommend professional property inspections to identify any potential issues or defects that may not be immediately apparent.

Disclosure Requirements: Sellers are required to disclose known material defects and issues that may affect the property's value or desirability.

Market Information

Market Data: Market data and pricing information are based on current market conditions and may change without notice. Past performance does not guarantee future results.

Valuation Estimates: Property valuations are estimates based on comparable sales and market analysis. Actual sale prices may vary based on market conditions and negotiation.

Investment Risks: Real estate investments carry inherent risks including market fluctuations, economic conditions, and regulatory changes.
